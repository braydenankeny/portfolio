Points

In Formula 1 there are 10 different teams competing for the constructors championship. Each team has 2 drivers that race for them. While these drivers are competing for the team, they are also competing individually for the drivers championship title at the same time. At the end of each race drivers get points based on what position they finished as well as an extra point for whichever driver had the fastest lap of the race. The bottom 10 drivers don't get any points. 1st place gets 25 points, second gets 18, third gets 15, and so on all the way down to the driver in 10th getting 1 point. The points from both drivers of a team are added to the teams score and at the end of the season, the F1 team with the most points gets crowned the constructors champion.

Teams and Drivers

The 10 teams that are competing in F1 are: Red Bull Racing, Ferrari, Mclaren, Mercedes, Aston Martin, RB, Haas, Alpine, Williams, and Kick Sauber. Max Verstappen and Sergio Perez race for Red Bull. Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz race for Ferrari. Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri race for Mclaren. Lewis Hamilton and George Russell race for Mercedes. Fernando Alonso and Lance Stroll race for Aston Martin. Yuki Tsunoda and Daniel Ricciardo for RB. Nico Hulkenberg and Kevin Magnussen for Haas. Pierre Gasly and Esteban Ocon for Alpine. Alex Albon and Logan Sargeant for Williams. Valtteri Bottas and Zhou Guany for Kick Sauber.
